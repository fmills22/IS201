Whipped Pavlova

A fruity New Zealand delicacy

Posted by Felicity Mills on April 10, 2024

This recipe is one near and dear to my heart. It was given to me by my sweet grandmother, Mignon Mills. I called her Grandma Nani. Althoug the dessert is New Zealander, Nani is Danish. Nani grew up right here in Provo Utah. Her grandfather was a professor of the Old Testament at Brigham Young University. Although she was raised in the valley, the mountains were here home. Upon retirement, her and her sweetheart moved into a rustic mountain home nestled deep in Big Cottonwood Canyon. What few neighbors she had hailed from all over the globe, the most exotic of which being from New Zealand. Nani was a wonderful visiting teacher, and visited her New Zealander Neighbor often. Her kindness won her a lifelong friendship, as well as this coveted family recipe.

Whipped Pavlova


  • 4 Egg Whites
  • 1 Cup of Sugar
  • 1 Tsp Vinegar (White or Malt)
  • 1/2 Tsp Vanilla
  • 1 Tbsp Cornstarch
  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees
  2. Beat the egg whites for 5 to 6 minutes, gradually adding sugar, vinegar, and vanilla
  3. When mixture has stiffened, sift in cornstarch and fold in lightly
  4. Heap the mixture onto a foil lined baking tray so as to form a round shape
  5. When oven has preheated, lower the temperature to 250 degrees
  6. Bake dessert, undisturbed, for 1 1/2 hours
  7. Remove and allow to cool completely, then fold back the foil and place on a cake plate
  8. Decorate with whipped cream on top, then top with your choice of fruit (strawberries, kiwi, passion fruit, grated chocolate, and banana is my preference)

Aunt Ethel's Donuts

A sure and sweet way to your deep fried grave

Posted by Felicity Mills on April 11, 2024

This recipe comes from my great aunt Ethel. I don't know what was up in the 70s, but it seems to me that they were cooking with an inordinate amount of powdered milk. I'm saying it was a direct result of the drug experimentation and sexual revolution. Against all astronaut food odds, these donuts are delicious. They were a Sunday night staple at my Grandma Baker's house growing up and I continue to dazzle my husband with them on Sunday nights to this day.

Aunt Ethel's Donuts


  • 2 Tbsp Yeast
  • 2 Cups Warm Water
  • 1 Tsp Brown Sugar
  • 1/2 Cup Sugar
  • 1/2 Cup Butter
  • 2 1/2 Tsp Salt
  • 2/3 Cup Dry Milk
  • 1 Egg
  • 5-6 Cups Flour
  1. Combine the yeast, warm water, and brown sugar in a liquid measuring cup and let sit for 5 minutes
  2. Meanwhile combine the sugar, butter, salt, dry milk, egg, and flour in a large mixing bowl
  3. Add in the yeast mixture and knead until smooth and elastic (about 10 minutes)
  4. Place in greased bowl and let rise until doubled
  5. Punch down the dough, and allow to rise until doubled once more
  6. Roll out into a disk about 1/2 inch thick and cut out into donut shapes
  7. Let rise for about 1 hour
  8. Fry donuts in medium high grease, about 2 minutes on each side

Homemade Banana Cake

A mock healthy dessert with decadent chocolate frosting

Posted by Felicity Mills on April 12, 2024

This banana cake recipe is another Grandma Nani recipe. I'm not entirely sure why Banana cake/bread are such classic Utah staples. It doesn't feel very intuitive considering we live in a landlocked, non-tropical state. I don't know why we have adopted the banana so feriously, but I'm not mad about it. This cake is absolutely delicious.

Homemade Banana Cake


  • 1/2 Cup Nueral Oil
  • 1 1/2 Cup Sugar
  • 2 1/4 Cup Flour
  • 1 Tsp Salt
  • 1 Tsp Baking Soda
  • 1 1/2 Tsp Baking Powder
  • 3/4 Cup Buttermilk
  • 3 Ripe Bananas
  • 3/4 Cup Water
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Tsp Vanilla
  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine oil, sugar, flour, salt, baking soda, baking powder, and buttermilk until smooth
  3. Add in the bananas and 1/2 cup of water, and beat for 2 minutes
  4. Add in the remaining water, eggs, and vanilla and beat for another 2 minutes
  5. Pour mixture into greased 8" cake pans and bake for 25-30 minutes
  6. Let cool completely, then stack and ice with chocolate buttercream frosting
